Need 2 — Use-Case Governance · Step 2 of 3

Pedagogical Context

These four questions map directly to the Cathedral layers and are required for a meaningful evaluation.

1
The Cornerstone — Values & SAMR Magis Test Required
Efficiency vs. Magis · Substitution vs. Redefinition
Is this doing "the same thing faster" (Substitution) or "a genuinely new thing better" (Redefinition/Magis)?
→ Does this use serve the Magis, or substitute human effort without formative value?
2
The Scriptorium — Pedagogy & Bloom's / IPP Required
Cognitive load · Pilot vs. Passenger · Mandatory reflection
Is the student the thinker, or is the AI doing the thinking?
→ Is there a mandatory reflection or Ignatian Examen step built into this use?
3
The Ambo — Doctrine & Truth Standard Required
Studiousness vs. Sloth · Hallucination risk · Developmental appropriateness
Does this promote the pursuit of truth, or the avoidance of effort?
→ Would a student become more capable or more dependent from this use?
4
The Bell Tower — Transparency Required
Parental disclosure · Explainability · Radical honesty
If a parent asks "Did AI help my son?" — can you answer clearly and confidently?
→ Is there a clear, honest answer ready for parents, students, and administration?

Need 3 — Institutional Alignment · Step 2 of 3

Mission & Institutional Context

These four questions map institutional AI use against Belen's Catholic identity, governance principles, and operational responsibilities. All four are required.

1
Human Dignity & Cura Personalis Required
Does this preserve the irreplaceable human relationship at the core of the work?
Belen's mission centers on forming persons, not processing transactions. Does this AI use preserve the dignity of those affected — employees, families, students — or does it reduce them to data inputs?
→ Could the people affected — if they knew AI was involved — feel that their dignity and individual worth were honored?
2
Human Governance & Accountability Required
Is a named, qualified person responsible for every output that has institutional consequence?
Belen's AI Guiding Principles require that no final decision be made by AI alone. Who reviews, who approves, who is accountable?
→ Is there a clear human decision-maker who can be named, who has the authority and expertise to override the AI at every step?
3
Data Stewardship & Legal Compliance Required
FERPA · COPPA · employee privacy · confidentiality obligations
What categories of data will the AI process? Is this data type covered by Belen's existing DPAs? Could this create liability or reputational risk?
→ If the AI vendor were audited tomorrow, would Belen's data handling hold up legally and reputationally?
4
Institutional Witness & Transparency Required
Is Belen comfortable being publicly known to use AI this way?
Belen is a Catholic institution that trains young men to be ethical leaders. Its institutional practices are a form of witness. Would this use, if disclosed to families, alumni, the Board, or the Diocese, reflect the institution's stated values?
→ Would the President or Principal be comfortable explaining this use at a Board meeting or in a family communication?
